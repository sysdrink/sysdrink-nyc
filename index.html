About

Sysdrink NYC - we’re an informal local sysadmin meetup where Ops Engineers, DevOps, Sysadmins, SREs, (and anything else we end up calling ourselves) gather and compare the current projects, vendors, infrastructures, and incidents that plague and amuse us. The group is heavy on startup people, but we're lucky enough to have some people in our group from scientific, financial, and government orgs, too.

As CoffeeOps points out, in tech, “you need regular infusions of external input”. Are you considering containerizing, microservices, or IP-over-Avian implementations? There's probably several of us in the middle of the same projects. Save yourself some effort duplicaton by talking it out.

Come and talk about your upcoming presentation, the last DevOpsDays, writing your first public-facing postmortem, a config management class, walking nontechnicals through version control, malfunctioning metrics tools, hardware and software vendors you love or want to strangle, or how you’ve had to wrap unmaintained abandonware in a restart script.

Sponsors

Sysdrink NYC was started in the spirit of the roving cross-startup engineering lunch in the Flatiron area of Manhattan. We generally meet at a small bar, take over a room, and frequently have a sponsor covering about $1-2K of drinks and snacks. We’re starting to try alternating months at non-alcohol venues, both for variety and to improve inclusivity. There’s no formal structure, no topics, no presentations. Instead we try to meet each other and commiserate about our stacks, and get help on our painpoints. If you’re the sponsoring company and you’re a vendor, it is HIGHLY recommended you bring your most technical sales engineers and customer service people - the people the sysadmins would want to talk with! Sponsoring a Sysdrink is also a good way to fill a req - we’ve been hiring each other from this pool for a few years now. Recruiters from companies who aren’t at least with the monthly sponsor aren’t welcome, sorry.
